Five steps to your first content sprint

The system keeps the setup lean: voice first, goals second, channels third, then it generates the first execution plan.

Step 01

Teach the platform how you sound

Start with voice DNA. The system uses this to keep hooks, scripts, captions, and repurposed assets aligned with your natural tone.

Upload past scripts, newsletters, or captions to build the voice fingerprint. Use one strong voice description instead of multiple contradictory tone settings.
Step 02

Import the knowledge that powers better content

Connect notebooks, docs, and archives so the Second Brain can pull context from your existing ideas instead of generating generic drafts.

Notion workspace

Idea backlog, research hub, and publishing notes.

Google Drive

Recorded transcripts, thumbnails, sponsor notes, and outlines.

Podcast archive

Optional but valuable for long-form repurposing and voice learning.

Newsletter history

Improves cross-format consistency and narrative continuity.

Step 03

Set the growth goal and autonomy level

Creators Pod translates high-level ambition into content sprints, cadence, and feedback loops. This determines what the next 30 to 90 days look like.

Autonomy level
Assistive Co-pilot Autopilot
Step 04

Connect channels and scheduling tools

Choose where the system can draft, queue, and publish. You can let it automate research while keeping publish actions gated for review.

YouTube

Long-form scripts, shorts derivatives, and metadata optimization.

TikTok

Trend-aligned short-form concepts, hook testing, and cadence planning.

LinkedIn

Thought-leadership posts and performance-informed authority building.

Substack

Optional long-form repurposing for audience ownership.

Step 05

Launch the first autonomous week

The platform now has enough signal to propose a real first sprint. Review the generated output, approve anything that should go live, and let the loop begin.

Planned output 2 YouTube scripts, 4 LinkedIn posts, 6 short-form hooks, 1 repurposing brief.
Learning loop Performance data will tune future hooks, timing, and content pillar priority.
